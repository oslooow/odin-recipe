How to make Gudeg

Nasi Goreng is a Southeast Asian fried rice dish, usually cooked with pieces of meat and vegetables. One of Indonesia's national dishes, it is also eaten in Malay-speaking communities in countries such as Malaysia, Singapore and Brunei, and has gained popularity in Sri Lanka through migrations from the Malay Archipelago.

The Ingredients

  • 500 gr steamed rice
  • 500 gr dried chilies (soak in hot water then drain)
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 shallots
  • 4 tbsp cooking oil
  • 50 gr chicken liver (diced and blanched)
  • 50 gr chicken gizzard (diced and blanched)
  • 100 gr chicken breast (cut and blanched)
  • ½ cup cabbage (thinly sliced)
  • ½ cup young spinach
  • 3 eggs
  • 50 gr of chives
  • 3 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  1. Combine dry chilies, garlic and shallots in a skillet.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of oil to a skillet over medium heat.
  3. After the hot oil, fry the chili sauce until fragrant.
  4. In the same skillet, add chicken breast and liver, then saute until well blended.
  5. In a different skillet, fry the chicken gizzards and then place it in the skillet containing the mixture of liver and chicken meat.
  6. Add the cabbage slices and spinach leaves, saute until wilted.
  7. Add rice, 3 tablespoons sweet soy sauce, 1 tablespoon fish sauce, 1 tablespoon white pepper, and 1 tablespoon salt.
  8. Fry over high heat and mix until evenly distributed. Mix the eggs in a bowl. Add the eggs to the pan, first transferring the rice to the edge of the pan.
  9. Stir the eggs over high heat to make a moist, scrambled egg, mix thoroughly with the rice.
  10. For something different, you can also fry a thin omelette to cover the rice.
